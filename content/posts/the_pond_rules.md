Welcome to the pond[^title-ref].

Here, I list some rules that **I** will follow when I post things on the blog, but you are not obliged to follow them at all.

### No. 1 Writing for fun
As one of my favorite hackers [Robert Nystrom](https://journal.stuffwithstuff.com/) put it:

> I don’t think ornithology books worry about justifying their existence. They assume the reader loves birds and start teaching.

I write things down not because it's *useful*, but simply because I think it's fun.

### No. 2 Responsible use of AI
Every word and every line of code originates from me. AI[^ai] may help me correct my writing, but never generates the content itself.

### No. 3 Creative contribution
Some of my projects are inspired by or based on others' work. In addition to clarifying the source, each project I post will meet at least one of the following criteria:
1. Solve the problem with a different approach or implementation.
2. Add new features.
3. Apply an approach to a different problem.


[^title-ref]: The title *The Pond Rules* is a reference to the 2026 film [*Hoppers*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hoppers_(film)).
[^ai]: Here, the term *AI* mostly means "generative AI" like ChatGPT and Claude.
